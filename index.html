Bad Things → Good People?

QUIZ

What kind of person are you?

Please choose the answer that best suits how you would respond in each scenario below:

1. Someone comes up to you and says “ You’re pure good”, what would you reply:
         a.  No one is all good, except of course: God. (And I know for a fact that I ain’t him)
         b.  Thank you.
         c.  Of course, I am amazing. (wait they said pure gold right, because I feel like a million bucks)

2. You win a million dollars. What is the first thing you do?
         a.  Thank God, because he made this happen.
         b.  Put it in your savings, eventually I can choose if I want to donate or keep it to myself.
         c.  Spend it on luxury items for yourself (A new car sounds nice)

3. Someone says “Zebra's are black with white stripes”. How do you react?
         a.  Listen to their reasoning; maybe they have a valid reasoning.
         b.  Don’t say anything. Better to stay quiet than to stir a controversy.
         c.  “Let me stop you there I know for a fact that zebras are black with white stripes”

4. Someone asks you for a favor. What goes on in your mind.
         a.  Of course; I would help.
         b.  If I help them they might pay me or return the favor one day.
         c.  Are they gonna pay me otherwise I'm not gonna help.

5. You’re told to sit by a fountain and to have patience and God will talk to you. What do you do in this scenario:
         a.  Question the person asking me to see if they are serious. If convinced of their intentions, wait by the fountain.
                                (they never said I couldn’t play candy crush on my phone while waiting)
         b.  Leave, there isn’t a toilet here and I don’t want to be peeing while talking to God
         c.  Its been 3 hours and I am not that patient. This person lied to me. I’m done.

RESULTS

ANSWERS

If your answers are mainly:
a = Good
b = Neutral
c = Misguided

But regardless of what you got remember:
“For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life. For God did not send his Son into the world to condemn the world, but in order that the world might be saved through him.” (John 3:16-17)

MODERN DAY GOOD

Captain America (Marvel Cinematic Universe)
Known to stand up for those who need it without asking people to return a favor. Always willing to make a sacrifice and guide people back to the righteous path.

Corresponding Answers: "a"

  1. No one is all good, except of course: God. (And I know for a fact that I ain’t him)
  2. Thank God, because he made this happen.
  3. Listen to their reasoning; maybe they have a valid reasoning.
  4. Of course; I would help.
  5. Question the person asking me to see if they are serious. If convinced of their intentions, wait by the fountain.

Why?

  1. Accepting that no one can be pure good except for God. (LUKE 18:19)
  2. All good things come from God. (JAMES 1:17)
  3. Be quick to listen (JAMES 1:19-20)
  4. There is a value to those who help without asking for a reward (PHILIPPIANS 2:3-4 )
  5. Many people will lie about their identity and their true reasons, to question is only fair. The real challenge is to understand patience (JAMES 5:8)

SO, WHY DO BAD THINGS HAPPEN TO GOOD PEOPLE?

  • Well, there are many reasons. Ultimately though, no human can accurately assess why a particular event happened. Only God can.
  • “Now we see things imperfectly, like puzzling reflections in a mirror, but then we will see everything with perfect clarity. All that I know now is partial and incomplete, but then I will know everything completely, just as God now knows me completely.” (Corinthians 13:12)

  • Romans tells us tells us that it's not even about being "glorious" or sin-free; it's about faith. So while the above quiz is full of satire, the real take-home is to honor God and be faithful.
  • "But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been made known, to which the Law and the Prophets testify. This righteousness is given through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe. There is no difference between Jew and Gentile, for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus. God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his forbearance he had left the sins committed beforehand unpunished— he did it to demonstrate his righteousness at the present time, so as to be just and the one who justifies those who have faith in Jesus." (Romans 3:21-26)

  • To go off of that, Romans includes another great passage. Specifically, where the author says “I consider that our present sufferings are not worth comparing with the glory that will be revealed in us.”. This is a great point that our real focus should not be on the "bad" in the present, but rather the blessing that follows.
  • "I consider that our present sufferings are not worth comparing with the glory that will be revealed in us. 19 For the creation waits in eager expectation for the children of God to be revealed. 20 For the creation was subjected to frustration, not by its own choice, but by the will of the one who subjected it, in hope 21 that[h] the creation itself will be liberated from its bondage to decay and brought into the freedom and glory of the children of God."(Romans 8:18-21)

  • Following the previous passage in Romans, it says that God works all things for the good of those who love him. This doesnt say that bad things won't happen to them. As mentioned above, we cannot see the full picture. This is God making a promise to faithful individuals that he will make bad things ultimately good; HOWEVER, he doesn't say that bad things will not happen.
  • "And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose. For those God foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son, that he might be the firstborn among many brothers and sisters. And those he predestined, he also called; those he called, he also justified; those he justified, he also glorified."(Romans 8:28-30)
